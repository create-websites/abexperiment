"Potential Outcomes: A/B Testing Results Explained"

Potential Outcomes

Outcome Description Impact on A/B Testing
Positive When the treatment group experiences a statistically significant improvement compared to the control group. Indicates that the treatment is effective and should be implemented.
Negative When the treatment group experiences a statistically significant decline compared to the control group. Indicates that the treatment is ineffective or harmful and should not be implemented.
Null When there is no statistically significant difference between the treatment and control groups. Indicates that the treatment has no effect and further testing or changes are needed.
Uninterpretable When there is not enough data or the data is inconclusive to make a determination. Indicates that more data or a different approach is needed to make a determination.
