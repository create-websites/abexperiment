The 108 names of Lakshmi Mata, or the Ashtottara Shatanamavali, are a powerful tool for invoking the blessings of the Goddess of wealth, prosperity, and abundance. Each name represents a specific aspect of Her divine nature, qualities, and powers.

Significance of the 108 Names:

  • Divine Attributes: Names highlighting Her divine nature, such as Mahalakshmi (Great Goddess of Wealth), Adi Lakshmi (Primal Goddess of Wealth), Sri Maha Lakshmi (Goddess of Great Fortune).
  • Powers and Abilities: Names emphasizing Her powers, such as Samruddhidayini (Bestower of Abundance), Apradhakshyami (Forgiver of Sins), Kalyanidayini (Bestower of Welfare).
  • Symbolism: Names associated with symbolic objects, such as Padmavathi (Goddess of the Lotus), Srivatsa-dhari (Bearer of the Srivatsa Mark), Bhagyada Lakshmi (Goddess of Fortune).
  • Manifestations: Names representing Her various manifestations, such as Adhi Lakshmi (Goddess of Prosperity), Dhana Lakshmi (Goddess of Wealth), Vidya Lakshmi (Goddess of Knowledge).

Benefits of Reciting the 108 Names:

  • Invoking Prosperity: Reciting the 108 names of Lakshmi Mata can attract wealth, abundance, and financial success.
  • Granting Blessings: Lakshmi Mata is believed to bestow blessings on devotees in various aspects of life, including health, happiness, and prosperity.
  • Removing Negativity: Reciting Her names can help remove negative energy and obstacles that hinder progress.
  • Spiritual Growth: Reciting the 108 names can deepen one's connection with the Divine Feminine and foster spiritual growth.

How to Use the 108 Names:

  • Mantra Japa: Repeat the 108 names in a rhythmic manner, often using a mala (prayer beads).
  • Stotra Recitation: Recite the names as part of a larger prayer or hymn.
  • Meditation: Focus on the meaning and energy of each name during meditation.

Using the 108 Names on Diwali:

Diwali, the Festival of Lights, is an auspicious time to invoke the blessings of Lakshmi Mata. Here are some ways to use the 108 names during this festival:

  • Chant the Names: Devotees can chant the 108 names of Lakshmi Mata throughout the Diwali celebrations, especially on Dhanteras and Diwali night.
  • Offer Prayers: Offer prayers to Lakshmi Mata using the 108 names, seeking Her blessings for wealth, prosperity, and happiness.
  • Decorate with Lakshmi Symbols: Decorate your home with symbols associated with Lakshmi Mata, such as the lotus, the conch, and the Srivatsa mark.
  • Perform Lakshmi Puja: Perform a Lakshmi Puja on Diwali night, using the 108 names as part of the ritual.

By incorporating the 108 names of Lakshmi Mata into your Diwali celebrations, you can invite Her blessings into your life and create a positive and prosperous atmosphere.
