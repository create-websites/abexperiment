"Decoding One-Sided Compliance in Experiments"

One-sided compliance in field experiments occurs when only the treatment group has the option to adhere to an intervention, requiring researchers to account for compliance variation to accurately estimate causal effects using methods like instrumental variable analysis.


Concept Description
One-Sided Compliance in Field Experiments
One-sided compliance in the context of field experiments refers to a scenario where only one group, typically the treatment group, has the option or opportunity to comply with the intervention or treatment being studied. Participants in the treatment group may choose to adhere to the intervention (compliers) or not (non-compliers), whereas the control group is never exposed to the intervention and thus has no opportunity to comply or deviate. This concept is particularly important for analyzing the impact of interventions, as researchers must account for the variation in compliance within the treatment group to accurately estimate the causal effect. Statistical methods such as instrumental variable analysis are often used to isolate the treatment effect among compliers, ensuring robust and meaningful conclusions.

The Key Simplification: In **one-sided noncompliance**, the control group can't get the treatment. This means Always-Takers and Defiers are impossible, leaving us with just Compliers and Never-Takers!

What Are We Actually Measuring?

With noncompliance, we can't just compare treated vs. untreated. We must choose the right causal question.

Intention-to-Treat (ITT)

The effect of OFFERING the treatment.

This compares everyone assigned to treatment vs. everyone assigned to control. It's a real-world policy effect, but it's "diluted" because many people in the treatment group don't actually get treated.

Complier Average Causal Effect (CACE)

The effect of RECEIVING the treatment.

This measures the true effect of the treatment, but only for the sub-group of Compliers. It adjusts for the dilution to estimate the treatment's true efficacy on those who use it.
